History of Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, or “Palace of Winds,” is a striking five-story palace in Jaipur, Rajasthan, built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. Designed like a honeycomb with 953 small windows called “jharokhas,” it allowed royal women to observe street festivals and daily life without being seen, maintaining their privacy under the strict purdah system. Made of red and pink sandstone, the palace’s unique facade is both beautiful and functional—its latticework also lets cool breezes flow through, keeping the interiors pleasantly airy even during hot summers. The Hawa Mahal is a stunning example of Rajput architecture and one of Jaipur’s most popular tourist attractions.
