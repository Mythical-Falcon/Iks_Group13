Konark Sun Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage Site

The Magnificent Heritage

The Konark Sun Temple, located in Odisha, is a 13th-century architectural marvel dedicated to the Hindu sun god, Surya. Built by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty around 1250 CE, the temple is designed in the shape of a gigantic chariot with twelve intricately carved stone wheels and seven horses, symbolizing the movement of the sun across the sky. The detailed sculptures and reliefs on the temple walls depict scenes from daily life, mythology, and dance, showcasing the rich artistic heritage of medieval India. Despite partial ruin over centuries, the temple remains a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a stunning example of Kalinga architecture, attracting visitors and scholars worldwide who admire its spiritual and cultural significance. .
