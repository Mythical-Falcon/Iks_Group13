Red Fort, Delhi

History

The Red Fort, or Lal Qila, was built in 1648 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. It served as the political center of the Mughals and remains a powerful symbol of India’s independence. The fort is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a major tourist attraction in Delhi.

Timings

Opening Hours: 9:30 AM to 4:30 PM

Closed On: Mondays

Entry Fee

Indian Citizens: ₹35

Foreign Tourists: ₹500

Children (under 15): Free

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to explore the Red Fort is between October and March, when Delhi’s climate is most pleasant for sightseeing.
