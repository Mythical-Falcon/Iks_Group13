Ajanta Ellora Caves

A UNESCO World Heritage Site

Ancient Rock-Cut Splendor

The Ajanta Caves, dating from the 2nd century BCE, and the Ellora Caves from the 6th to 10th century CE, are famous for their rock-cut architecture and beautiful frescoes.

They reflect the peak of Indian art, featuring Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain monuments intricately carved into the rock face of the Sahyadri hills.
