Hawa Mahal

The Palace of Winds, Jaipur

History of Hawa Mahal

Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, the Hawa Mahal is an iconic landmark in Jaipur, Rajasthan. Known as the "Palace of Winds," its unique design allows cool breezes to flow through the building, providing comfort in the hot desert climate.

The palace is a stunning example of Rajput architecture, featuring 953 small windows or 'jharokhas' with intricate latticework, allowing royal women to observe life outside without being seen.
